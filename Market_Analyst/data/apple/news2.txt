Earnings call transcript: Apple Q1 2025 beats EPS forecast, stock rises
Apple Inc. reported its first-quarter earnings for fiscal year 2025, surpassing Wall Street's expectations for earnings per share (EPS) but slightly missing on revenue forecasts. The company's stock saw a positive reaction in aftermarket trading. Apple's performance was bolstered by strong growth in services and product innovations, although challenges in Greater China remained.

Key Takeaways
EPS of $2.40 exceeded the forecast of $2.36.
Revenue of $124.3 billion slightly below the expected $124.99 billion.
Aftermarket stock price increased by 3.11% to $244.99.
Record revenue across major geographic segments.
Continued strong growth in services revenue by 14% YoY.
Company Performance
Apple demonstrated robust performance in Q1 FY2025, with revenue reaching $124.3 billion, marking a 4% year-over-year increase. The company achieved record sales across the Americas, Europe, Japan, and the Rest of Asia Pacific. Despite a decline in Greater China revenue, Apple maintained its market leadership in the smartphone, tablet, and PC sectors, driven by new product launches and service expansions.

Financial Highlights
Revenue: $124.3 billion (+4% YoY)
EPS: $2.40 (+10% YoY)
Gross Margin: 46.9%
Net Income: $36.3 billion
Product Revenue: $98 billion (+2% YoY)
Services Revenue: $26.3 billion (+14% YoY)
Earnings vs. Forecast
Apple's EPS of $2.40 surpassed the forecast of $2.36, representing a positive surprise of approximately 1.7%. However, the company reported revenue of $124.3 billion, slightly below the anticipated $124.99 billion. This mixed result reflects Apple's ability to manage costs effectively, boosting earnings despite the revenue shortfall.


Market Reaction
Following the earnings announcement, Apple's stock rose by 3.11% in aftermarket trading, reaching $244.99. This positive movement contrasts with the day's regular session, where the stock closed down 0.74%. The stock remains within its 52-week range, with a high of $260.1 and a low of $164.08, indicating investor confidence in Apple's long-term strategy.

Outlook & Guidance
Apple expects revenue growth in the low to mid-single digits for the upcoming March quarter, with services revenue projected to increase by low double digits. The company anticipates a gross margin between 46.5% and 47.5%. Apple's strategic focus includes continued investment in AI and expanding its Apple Intelligence features.

Executive Commentary
CEO Tim Cook emphasized the company's commitment to innovation, stating, "We're always searching across a world of possibilities, finding those places where we can do the most good." CFO Kevin Perek highlighted Apple's extensive services portfolio, underscoring its role in driving revenue growth.

Q&A
During the earnings call, analysts inquired about the early adoption of Apple Intelligence and the company's performance in emerging markets. Apple noted strong upgrade cycles for its Mac and iPhone products and expressed optimism about its growth prospects in markets like India.

Risks and Challenges
Supply chain disruptions could impact product availability and costs.
Market saturation in mature regions may limit growth potential.
Macroeconomic pressures, such as inflation, could affect consumer spending.
Geopolitical tensions, particularly in China, pose risks to revenue.
Intense competition in the tech industry may pressure margins.
