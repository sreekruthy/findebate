Apple (AAPL) Q1 2025 Earnings
Overview
AAPL reported record Q1 2025 revenue and earnings driven by strong sales across various product lines and regions. Apple Intelligence and services business were key growth drivers, and the company continues to expand its global presence and product offerings.

Key Highlights
Record revenue of $124.3 billion, up 4% year-over-year.
EPS reached an all-time high of $2.40, up 10% year-over-year.
$26.3 billion in services revenue, a 14% increase year-over-year.
iPhone revenue of $69.1 billion, driven by adoption of Apple Intelligence.
Mac revenue rose 16% year-over-year to $9 billion, strong uptake of new Mac products.
iPad revenue up 15% year-over-year to $8.1 billion, led by iPad Air and entry models.
2.35 billion active devices in installed base, all-time high.
Expansion of Apple Intelligence and international market penetration.
Significant growth in emerging markets, including record sales in India.
Introduction of Apple Intelligence features and expansion to new markets.
Financial Performance
AAPL achieved a record Q1 2025 revenue of $124.3 billion, up 4% from the previous year, setting new highs in most regions. Earnings per share were also a record at $2.40, a 10% increase from the previous year. Gross margin stood at 46.9%, with product margins at 39.3% and services margins at 75%. The company's financial performance was bolstered by strong sales of iPhones, Macs, and iPads, alongside robust growth in its services segment.

Future Outlook
Management anticipates continued revenue growth in the low to mid-single digits for the next quarter, with services expected to grow in the low double digits despite foreign exchange headwinds. The expansion of Apple Intelligence to new languages and countries is expected to sustain growth in consumer engagement. Apple is optimistic about its prospects in emerging markets, especially India, and aims to continue innovation in product lines and services.
Q&A Highlights
CEO Tim Cook noted Apple Intelligence was a key factor driving iPhone upgrades and sales in markets where it launched.
China remains a challenging market due to macroeconomic factors and competitive pressures.
Apple's strategy in emerging markets, particularly India, includes expansion with new retail stores.
Question about the impact of AI and cost efficiencies on gross margins, with optimism for further innovation.
Discussion on the future innovation potential in iPhones and the confidence in the product pipeline.
Strong growth potential and momentum seen in Mac and iPad sales attributed to compelling M4 products.
Management acknowledged currency headwinds affecting future revenue growth and gross margins.
Discussion on product lifecycle and upgrade opportunities from post-COVID sales.
